# Founder Equity and Vesting Analysis

## Context

Flowser currently has two founders:

- Ky-Nam Nguyen: CEO, full-time on the company, owns product, frontend, distribution, GTM, content, and community.
- Zi Hao Lam: CTO, long-time cofounder, strong technical and infra background, currently working a full-time job and contributing around 10 hours/week.

The company is considering adding a third GTM cofounder, but fit is still being tested and that person will not join full-time immediately.

The previous split was 50/50 between Ky-Nam and Zi Hao. Given the current commitment difference, the proposed target split is:

- Ky-Nam: 60%
- Zi Hao: 30%
- New GTM cofounder: 10%

This should be treated as the fully vested target split, not immediate fully owned equity.

## Main Principle

Equity should match both:

- Past contribution
- Future full-time commitment

The cleanest structure is:

> Each founder's serious founder equity starts vesting when that founder becomes full-time.

Part-time contribution can earn small or past-contribution equity, but full founder equity should be tied to full-time commitment.

## Recommended Structure

## Ky-Nam: 60%

Ky-Nam has already been full-time for around 1 year.

- Grant: 60%
- Vesting schedule: 4 years total
- Vesting start date: backdated 12 months
- Cliff: already satisfied
- Immediately vested: 15%
- Remaining unvested: 45%
- Remaining vesting: monthly over the next 3 years

Reason:

Ky-Nam has already taken the full-time risk and carried the company while the others are part-time.

## Zi Hao: 30%

Zi Hao is still a core cofounder, but currently part-time.

Suggested split inside his 30%:

- 10%: treated as earned or vested for past contribution
- 20%: future founder equity tied to full-time commitment

For the remaining 20%:

- Vesting starts only when Zi Hao joins full-time
- Full-time trigger: company reaches $15k MRR or raises investment, including YC/seed investment
- After joining full-time: 4-year vesting with a 1-year cliff
- If the trigger happens and Zi Hao does not join full-time within 60-90 days, the unvested 20% should be cancelled or renegotiated

Reason:

Zi Hao should keep meaningful founder ownership because of the long history and technical contribution, but he should not vest future founder equity at the same speed while contributing only around 10 hours/week.

## New GTM Cofounder: 10%

The new cofounder is still being tested and will not join full-time immediately.

Suggested split inside the 10%:

- 2%: part-time trial grant
- 8%: full-time cofounder grant

For the 2% trial grant:

- 6-month cliff
- If they leave before 6 months: 0%
- If they stay and contribute past the cliff: equity starts vesting
- Suggested vesting: monthly over 12 months total
- Maximum part-time earned amount before full commitment: 2%

For the extra 8%:

- Granted only when they join full-time
- Full-time trigger can be the same: $15k MRR or YC/seed investment
- After joining full-time: 4-year vesting with a 1-year cliff

Reason:

The new person can earn a small amount while proving fit, but the full 10% should require full-time commitment.

## Cliff Explanation

A cliff means a person gets no vested equity unless they stay for a minimum period.

Example:

- Grant: 10%
- Vesting: 4 years
- Cliff: 1 year

If the person leaves after 11 months, they keep 0%.

If they stay for 12 months, they vest the first 25% of their grant.

After that, the rest usually vests monthly.

## Should the Cliff Start Only When Everyone Is Full-Time?

No.

It is better if each founder's main vesting starts when that specific founder becomes full-time.

Do not make one person's equity depend on whether the other founders are full-time. That creates unnecessary dependency and confusion.

Better rule:

> Ky-Nam's vesting has already started because he is already full-time. Zi Hao's future vesting starts when Zi Hao becomes full-time. The new GTM cofounder's full cofounder vesting starts when that person becomes full-time.

## Dilution During Vesting

Vesting and dilution are different.

Vesting controls how much equity someone earns and keeps if they leave.

Dilution controls how everyone's percentage shrinks when new investors or option pools are added.

Example before investors:

- Ky-Nam: 60%
- Zi Hao: 30%
- New GTM cofounder: 10%

If investors buy 10% of the company, everyone is diluted equally:

- Ky-Nam: 60% becomes 54%
- Zi Hao: 30% becomes 27%
- New GTM cofounder: 10% becomes 9%
- Investors: 10%

If YC takes 7%:

- Ky-Nam: 60% becomes 55.8%
- Zi Hao: 30% becomes 27.9%
- New GTM cofounder: 10% becomes 9.3%
- YC: 7%

Important:

Even unvested shares are often shown in the cap table, but if someone leaves, the company can repurchase or cancel the unvested shares.

Plain English:

> Vesting controls earning. Dilution controls shrinking.

## Suggested Plain-English Agreement

> Our intended long-term founder split is 60/30/10. Ky-Nam gets credit for already being full-time. Zi Hao keeps meaningful founder equity for past and ongoing contribution, but his future founder vesting depends on returning full-time after $15k MRR or investment. The new GTM cofounder earns only trial equity while part-time and receives the full cofounder grant only after proving fit and joining full-time. All future founder equity is subject to vesting and cliffs.

## Practical Next Steps

- Confirm whether the 60/30/10 target split feels emotionally acceptable to all founders.
- Decide exactly how much of Zi Hao's 30% is considered already earned.
- Decide whether the new GTM cofounder's trial grant is 1%, 2%, or 3%.
- Define "full-time" clearly, such as 40+ hours/week and no conflicting full-time job.
- Define the trigger clearly: $15k MRR or YC/seed investment.
- Define the deadline after trigger: usually 60-90 days to join full-time.
- Ask a startup lawyer to convert this into proper founder stock restriction, vesting, repurchase, and shareholder documents.

## Short Recommendation

Use 60/30/10 as the target fully vested split.

Give Ky-Nam backdated full-time vesting.

Give Zi Hao meaningful past-credit equity, but make future vesting depend on full-time commitment.

Give the new GTM cofounder a small trial grant now, then the full cofounder grant only after full-time commitment.
